# RAG Citation Guidelines & Edge Case Handling
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Target Agent:** Regulatory Analyst (`query_dma_rag` skill)
**System:** LUX (Legal Uncovering, eXplainable)

## Purpose
This document dictates how agents must interpret, cite, and format chunks retrieved from the Vertex AI Search Knowledge Base. Because regulatory texts like the Digital Markets Act (DMA) are highly interdependent, agents must strictly adhere to these guidelines to prevent legal hallucinations and ensure explainability.

---

## 1. Core Citation Principles

1. **Absolute Traceability:** Every legal claim, definition, or restriction generated by the agent MUST map back to a specific Article and Paragraph from the retrieved chunks.
2. **Zero Extrapolation:** Do not infer legal intent. If the retrieved text does not explicitly prohibit an action, state that the text is silent on the matter. 
3. **No External Knowledge:** Do not supplement the RAG output with pre-trained LLM knowledge regarding EU law, antitrust cases, or corporate fines unless it is explicitly present in the retrieved chunk.

---

## 2. Handling Edge Cases

### Edge Case A: Cross-References to Un-indexed Directives
* **Scenario:** The retrieved DMA text references another regulatory framework not indexed in the RAG database (e.g., *"This is without prejudice to obligations under Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)."*)
* **Action:**
    1. Quote the DMA article directly.
    2. Explicitly acknowledge the cross-reference.
    3. **Crucial:** State that the referenced framework (e.g., GDPR) is outside the current scope of the investigation. Do NOT attempt to summarize the un-indexed framework.
    4. *Example Output:* "Under DMA Article 5(2), gatekeepers face specific restrictions, though the text notes this is without prejudice to the GDPR. Note: GDPR compliance analysis is outside the current system scope."

### Edge Case B: Fragmented or Ambiguous Chunks
* **Scenario:** The Vertex Search tool returns a chunk that contains a relevant keyword but cuts off before completing the legal definition, or lacks the parent Article number.
* **Action:**
    1. Do not attempt to "fill in the blanks" using base LLM knowledge.
    2. Flag the ambiguity in the report draft. 
    3. *Example Output:* "The retrieved documentation mentions 'self-preferencing' in the context of ranking, but the specific legal threshold within the chunk is fragmented. Human legal review is required to verify the exact parameters in the full DMA text."

### Edge Case C: Zero Relevant Results (Null Retrieval)
* **Scenario:** The `query_dma_rag` tool returns chunks with a low similarity score, or the chunks are entirely unrelated to the search parameter.
* **Action:**
    1. Output a definitive "No Match" statement.
    2. Do not attempt to guess the answer.
    3. *Example Output:* "No relevant definitions or restrictions matching '[Search Parameter]' were found in the indexed DMA documentation. The system cannot perform a compliance mapping for this specific query."

---

## 3. Citation Formatting Standard

When a successful match is found, extract the exact verbiage and format it using the following structure in the markdown report:

> "[Exact Quote from RAG Chunk]" 
> — **Source:** Digital Markets Act, Article [X], Paragraph [Y]

**Contextual Application:** [1-2 sentences strictly tying the technical data from the API Inspector to the quote above. Use phrasing like "This observation intersects with..." rather than "This violates..."]

---

## 4. Mandatory Disclaimer
Regardless of the outcome (Success, Ambiguity, or Null), the final output generated by the Regulatory Analyst using this RAG data MUST conclude with the following exact string:

`***Disclaimer: This analysis is generated via automated regulatory mapping for research purposes only. It does not constitute binding legal counsel, and findings must be verified by a qualified human legal professional.***`
